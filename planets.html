Mercury

Mercury, it is the closest planet to the sun. It’s slightly larger than the Earth's moon with a radius of approximately 2439.7 kilometers. Even though it is the planet closest to the sun it's not the hottest one, that Venus. This is because Mercury has a thin exosphere that cannot hold the sun’s warmth. The planet formed about 4.5 billion years ago and is a terrestrial planet. Its surface is similar to the one our moon has. A day on Mercury, one complete rotation on its axis, takes about 59 Earth days due to its slow spin compared to Earth. Being the closest planet to the Sun, Mercury has the shortest year among all planets, completing an orbit in just 88 Earth days. Mercury has a diameter of nearly 5000 km and is only 58 million kilometers from the Sun. The planet does not have any moons and experiences surface temperatures ranging from +430°C to -170°C, with an average temperature of 180°C. Mercury has been known since ancient times, although its close position to the Sun makes it hard to observe and study in detail.
